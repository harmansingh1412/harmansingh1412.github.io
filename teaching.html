üéì Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy centers on inclusive pedagogy and experiential learning. I believe that effective education requires creating spaces where all students feel valued and empowered to contribute their unique perspectives. Through my courses, I strive to connect theoretical concepts with real-world applications, particularly emphasizing the importance of environmental justice and community engagement in geographic research.

I am committed to fostering critical thinking skills that enable students to analyze complex socio-environmental challenges and develop innovative solutions. My approach emphasizes collaborative learning, hands-on experience with research methods, and the development of both technical and communication skills.
