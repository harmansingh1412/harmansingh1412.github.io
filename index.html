About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Geography and Climate Science at The Pennsylvania State University, specializing in water governance and climate adaptation. My research focuses on examining policy barriers and institutional responses in urban water management, with a particular emphasis on Bengaluru, India.

My interdisciplinary approach combines geospatial analysis, natural language processing, and qualitative research methods to understand complex socio-environmental challenges. I am passionate about making research accessible and impactful for both academic and broader communities.

Research Interests

  • Water Governance: Policy analysis and institutional responses
  • Climate Adaptation: Urban resilience and disaster risk reduction
  • Environmental Justice: Equity in sustainability initiatives
  • Geospatial Analysis: GIS and remote sensing applications
  • Natural Language Processing: Media discourse analysis

Current Focus

Dissertation Research

Water Governance and Climate Adaptation in Bengaluru

Examining policy barriers and institutional responses to urban water challenges in one of India's fastest-growing cities. This research combines policy analysis, stakeholder interviews, and spatial analysis to understand governance dynamics.

Professional Philosophy

I believe in the power of interdisciplinary collaboration and community-engaged research to address complex environmental challenges. My work aims to bridge the gap between academic research and practical solutions, always with an eye toward equity and justice in environmental decision-making.

Through my teaching, research, and service, I strive to create inclusive spaces that center diverse perspectives and experiences, particularly those of underrepresented communities in geography and environmental sciences.
